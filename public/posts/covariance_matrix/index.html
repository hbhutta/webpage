Covariance Matrix

Definition:

Let $X \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}$. Refer to the $d$-th column (feature) of $X$ as $f_{d}$. The covariance matrix $\text{Cov}(X)$ is the following real, symmetric matrix:

$$ \text{Cov}(X) = \begin{bmatrix} \text{Cov}(f_{1}, f_{1}) & \text{Cov}(f_{1}, f_{2}) & \ldots & \text{Cov}(f_{1}, f_{d}) \\ \text{Cov}(f_{2}, f_{1}) & \text{Cov}(f_{2}, f_{2}) & \ldots & \text{Cov}(f_{2}, f_{d}) \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \text{Cov}(f_{d}, f_{1}) & \text{Cov}(f_{d}, f_{2}) & \ldots & \text{Cov}(f_{d}, f_{d}) \\ \end{bmatrix} $$

On a related note, the covariance $\text{Cov}(X,Y)$ of two random variables $X$ and $Y$ is defined as: $$ \text{Cov}(X,Y) = E((X-\mu_{X})(Y-\mu_{Y})) $$ Which itself is a generalization of the variance of a random variable $X$: $$ \text{Cov}(X,X) = E((X-\mu_{X})(X-\mu_{X})) = E((X-\mu_{X})^{2}) = \text{Var}(X) $$

This means that if we zero-mean (i.e. “center”) the data matrix $X$, such that $X := X - \mu_{X}$, then: $$ \text{Cov}(X) = \frac{1}{n-1}X^{T}X $$
